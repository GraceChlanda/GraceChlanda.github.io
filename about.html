This is my Service Dog Journey

I am a twenty-two-year-old, disabled, college student. My sophomore year I was having some problems doing majojr life activities. So, I adopted my dog Josephine (Jojo) to be an Emotional Support Animal. Then, I spent another two years training her to be my service dog.
My family has always owned dogs growing up. Additionally, I have spent a lot of time working at a dog kennel and a professional dog walker. When I adopted Jojo, I spent hours a day for months learning about service dog training, and holding to the adage:

"There are no stubborn dogs."
‐Donna Hill

I learned from hundreds of online videos as well as forums. I also had the opportunity to take several college courses on the principles of learning as well as several professional training seminars. Recently, I have been able to work aside several local service dog trainers.
With these experiences me and my dog became a successful team. In the meantime, I lead a Facebook group for service dog handlers in Columbia, Missouri where we offer training help to anyone who needs it.
